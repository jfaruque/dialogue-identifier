Heart

Regulus rounds the corner at the same time as James and they run right into each other sending all their books scattering to the floor. 

“Sorry, I didn’t mean to–” James starts as they both bend down to pick up their things.

“Watch it, Potter!” Regulus says, but then freezes when he gets a glimpse of what’s in his hands. James freezes also.

Regulus is holding James’ notebook and it’s open to a page filled with his name. Regulus’ name. In various forms.

“Regulus Arcturus Black” surrounded by little stars.

A heart with “R. A. B.” in the middle.

A drawing of the Leo Constellation, the Regulus star significantly bigger than the others.

But it’s what’s at the bottom of the page that catches Regulus’ eye the most.

“Regulus Potter”

“Regulus Arcturus Potter”

“James Black”

But “James Black” is crossed out several times with the word “blah” written beside it. It makes Regulus chuckle a bit and he looks up at James with a raised eyebrow.

“I can explain.” James tries to tell him, and his face is bright red.

“So…” Regulus starts slowly. “You don’t like my last name? What’s wrong with ‘Black’?” Regulus asks.

“No. Nothing. I like your last name. I just–” James stumbles over his words.

“I mean, you’re right.” Regulus shrugs “It’s not a great last name. Plus… ‘Regulus Potter’ has a nice ring to it.” He smirks and James freezes again.

Regulus doesn’t wait for James to respond, he just turns on his heel and walks away. It’s a few moments before James seems to find his words again.

“Hey! My notebook!!” James calls after him.

But Regulus just keeps walking with a soft smile as he runs his fingers over the heart with his initials. Yeah, James is never getting that notebook back.

 

 

Rain

Cigarette

“You have beautiful eyes.” Regulus tells him.

“Tha… wait what?!” James sputters then just stares at Regulus dumbfounded.

“Bye James.” He says cheekily over his shoulder as James stands frozen at the railing.

Curl

Rainy days with Regulus are some of James’ favorite days. They make hot chocolate, put on a random movie, curl up on the couch together and forget the rest of the world exists.

Rainy days are normally James’ favorite, except today he’s in a fight with Regulus. It’s not a big fight, it’s just that Regulus is mad at James because he won’t apologize, and James is mad at Regulus because he can’t admit that’s he wrong. Regulus knows he’s wrong and James knows he should apologize, but they’re both too stubborn and now it’s just a point of pride.

So now it’s raining, and James is sitting on the couch alone trying to find a random movie to watch. He hears Regulus walk into the living room and he turns to look at him. Regulus doesn’t move any further into the room and he doesn’t say anything, he just narrows his eyes at James.

After about a minute of staring at each other Regulus seems to make a decision and shuffles across the room. He grabs a blanket, curls up on the couch resting his head on James’ lap and opens his book without saying a word.

After a moment, James decides to break the silence.

“Regulus? Are we–?” He starts cautiously.  

“I’m still mad at you, but I missed you, so play with my hair and let me read my book.” Regulus snaps but there’s very little heat behind it.

“Yes, love.” James says with a smile. He starts running his fingers through Regulus’ hair and puts on a random movie with the volume low so Regulus can still read his book.  

Later that night, while they’re curled up together listening to the rain, James will apologize to Regulus and Regulus will admit that he was “maybe, potentially, slightly less than accurate.”
